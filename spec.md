we're starting a project. I want to build a mobile-first, desktop friendly web based game that is multiplayer, social, and can be played with one finger on mobile. the idea is one of bumper tanks – each player has a somewhat abstract tank that rotates in the direction that the player clicks and fires. firing the cannon launches a projectile, which itself pushes the player's tank back in the opposite direction. on a direct impact, the projectile imparts some force on the tank it, on indirect impact (for example on a wall) the projectile explodes and imparts a smaller force on nearby tanks. the maps themselves are not bordered, a tank getting pushed off the side falls off, incurring a few seconds' penalty and counting as one death, or kill for the last other player to hit it. We could have two modes, one that's last person standing, the other where you just have to score as much as possible in the time limit. Also include a testing playground where I can spawn tanks and play. Design a few levels that are randomly selected, so that the game is engaging. Ensure the viewport width+height is considered when determining render scale, so every player can see a roughly equal amount of the map around their tank. The player's tank stays centered in the viewport, and tapping/clicking around the tank moves the map relative to the player's tank.

Use browser-native technologies as much as possible. Choose an aesthetic and commit hard to it. Multiplayer should use WebRTC, no need for a signaling server – generate a client ID randomly and render a QR code to share a lobby for now. Ideally this app runs entirely clientside.
